Effects

Liquid glass
Phones default to glass on; wider screens default off for speed. Turn on anytime for blur, glow, and softer chrome.
Snappy mode
On by default — fewer animated layers, lighter hovers, less GPU work. Turn off if you want the full motion polish.

FAQ

Guest vs Google sign-in?

Guest keeps data on this device only. Google syncs encrypted planner data to your account so you can use multiple devices.

Will I lose data?

Clearing browser data or uninstalling the browser can remove local guest data. Sign in with Google to sync your planner to the cloud across devices.

What does AI see?

Flux AI uses your chat and optional planner context you send. See the Privacy Policy for details.

For schools & families

Quick facts for administrators, counselors, and parents. Flux is a voluntary student planner—it does not replace official school systems (Canvas, SIS, email from the district).

  • Voluntary use. Students choose whether to use it. Teachers and staff are not required to adopt or support it.
  • Data. Planner data can stay on the device (guest) or sync to a signed-in account (cloud). Optional connections (e.g. Google, Canvas) only use what the student authorizes. Details are in the Privacy Policy.
  • AI. AI answers can be wrong—double-check anything you rely on.
  • Audience. Built for high school students; younger users should use it with a parent or guardian and school policy in mind.
  • Accessibility. Look → High contrast, font scaling, and reduce motion help usability; we welcome feedback on barriers.
  • Reliability. Works best online. Some features need sync or integrations; plan critical deadlines in official school tools too.
